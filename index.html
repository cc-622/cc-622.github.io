About

Background

I am a Ph.D. student in Coordination Chemistry at Kyushu University. I previously completed an MSc in Chemistry at the University of Manchester with Distinction.

My work focuses on molecular catalysis for CO2 reduction, combining mechanistic DFT studies and electrochemical experiments to design Ni-NHC systems with improved selectivity and practical solubility.

Specialties

Research

Research Interests

Ni-NHC Catalysis for Selective Formate Production

Mechanistic and design studies on Ni-NHC complexes to improve CO2-to-formate selectivity.

Redox Tuning Through Ion Pairing

Investigating ion-pair effects to control catalytic pathways in electrocatalytic CO2 reduction.

Water-Soluble Molecular Catalyst Design

Developing TEG-functionalized Ni-NHC systems with tunable solubility and electrochemical behavior.
